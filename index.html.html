Paper/Patent Publications

### Paper/Patent Publications I authored a research paper titled THE Black hole on Gravitational Pull: Black holes have an incredibly strong gravitational pull, so strong that not even light can escape once it crosses the event horizon. Formation: They typically form from the remnants of massive stars after they exhaust their nuclear fuel and undergo a supernova explosion. Types: There are several types of black holes: stellar (formed from stars), supermassive (found at the centers of galaxies, containing millions to billions of solar masses), and intermediate (with masses between stellar and supermassive). Spaghettification: The intense gravitational gradient near a black hole can stretch and compress objects in a process known as spaghettification, where they are elongated into thin shapes. Hawking Radiation: Proposed by physicist Stephen Hawking, this theoretical radiation suggests that black holes can emit particles and eventually evaporate over incredibly long timescales. Time Dilation: Near a black hole, time behaves differently due to intense gravitational effects. To an outside observer, time appears to slow down for anything approaching a black hole. Observation: While black holes are invisible, their presence can be inferred by observing the effects they have on nearby stars and gas, such as accelerated motion or X-ray emissions from accreting matter. First Image: In 2019, the Event Horizon Telescope captured the first image of a black hole's event horizon in the galaxy M87, providing a groundbreaking visual confirmation of their existence., which was published in The Astrophysical journal.
